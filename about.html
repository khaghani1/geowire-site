Primary Data Sources

FRED (St. Louis Federal Reserve)

Primary source for all US macro series: interest rates, yield curves, CPI, unemployment, housing starts, mortgage rates.

Bloomberg / Reuters

Commodity prices, equity indices, corporate credit spreads, market news. Confirmed = 2+ outlets agree. Multi-source = Bloomberg + one other.

Kalshi / Polymarket

Prediction market odds for recession probability, ceasefire timing, and oil price targets. Treated as market-implied probabilities, not forecasts.

Kpler / Clarksons

Strait of Hormuz tanker flow data, shipping route disruption estimates, and global commodity logistics.

CENTCOM / DoD

Official US military casualty figures, operational updates. Marked "Confirmed" when cited directly. Propaganda risk noted.

HRANA / OCHA / UN

Human Rights Activists News Agency for Iranian casualties. UN OCHA for displacement figures. Access-restricted — likely undercounts.

IEA / EIA

Global energy supply/demand balance, US gasoline prices, strategic petroleum reserve data.

Model Philosophy

Transparency over false precision. We show you every score, every weight, and every source. A 42% probability is not a guarantee — it's the output of a model with known limitations. We show the confidence range so you can calibrate appropriately.

The Iran conflict is an input, not the whole picture. Geopolitical shocks accelerate pre-existing macro trends. Our model scored the US economy at elevated recession risk before the conflict began. The war is a shock amplifier — not the sole cause.
