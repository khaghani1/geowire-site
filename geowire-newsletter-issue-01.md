## WHAT'S ACTUALLY HAPPENING

### The Military Picture

The coalition's air campaign has been more precise than any conflict in history, but precision hasn't translated to decisive outcomes. The core problem: Fordow.

Iran's main uranium enrichment facility is buried under 80 meters of rock near Qom. The US's largest conventional bunker-buster, the GBU-57 Massive Ordnance Penetrator, can penetrate roughly 60 meters. Coalition planners are debating repeated strikes to collapse tunnel networks — a strategy that takes time and carries escalation risk.

Meanwhile, Iran's IRGC Navy has turned Hormuz into a minefield. 23% of transit lanes are now mined or under active threat. Global shipping insurance has increased 1,800% for Gulf routes.

### The Proxy Network

This conflict is not US vs. Iran. It's a seven-front war:

- **Red Sea / Bab-el-Mandeb:** Houthi ASBMs have struck 14 vessels. Suez Canal traffic is down 60%. Cargo is rerouting via the Cape of Good Hope, adding 10–14 days and ~$1M per voyage.
- **Lebanon:** Hezbollah is exchanging daily fire with the IDF but hasn't opened a full front. Hassan Nasrallah is holding back his 150,000-rocket arsenal as leverage — not deploying it.
- **Iraq:** PMF factions have conducted 7 drone attacks on US bases at Ain al-Asad and Erbil. 3 US personnel wounded. The Baghdad government is unable (or unwilling) to stop them.

### The Diplomatic Deadlock

The UNSC has been completely neutralized. Russia vetoed the first ceasefire resolution (Day 4). China vetoed the second (Day 19). Both countries are benefiting economically from the disruption.

Qatar is hosting the most credible back-channel: quiet talks between an IRGC envoy and US NSC representatives. A 72-hour humanitarian pause was proposed by Doha and rejected by Tehran on Day 18.

## WHAT TO WATCH THIS WEEK

**1. Hezbollah's decision point.** Nasrallah has been unusually quiet. Every week he holds back, his deterrent value increases. At some point, Iran will call in the favor. Watch for unusual military movements in Lebanon's Bekaa Valley.

**2. Congressional authorization.** The administration is operating under an emergency War Powers Resolution. Congress has 60 days from the start of hostilities to authorize or end the conflict. Day 60 = April 29. The vote will be close.

**3. India and Turkey.** Both abstained at the UNSC. Both are energy-exposed and running their own diplomatic tracks. An India-Turkey joint mediation proposal would be a significant escalation in diplomatic activity.

**4. The oil SPR question.** The US, IEA, and Gulf states have released emergency oil reserves. But SPR capacity has limits. If releases aren't matched by increased production, prices could spike to $130+ before June.
